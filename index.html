Welcome to FleetExhibit

Discover the history of World War II naval vessels.

About Us

The FleetExhibit is a dedicated online resource showcasing the various naval vessels that played significant roles during World War II. Its primary purpose is to educate visitors about the history, design, and impact of these ships on naval warfare and the war's overall outcome. Overall, the World War 2 Navy Ship Gallery aims to preserve and promote the legacy of these ships, honoring the contributions of the naval forces during one of history's most significant conflicts.

Yamato

The IJN Yamato was the lead ship of the Yamato class of battleships in the Imperial Japanese Navy, launched in 1940. Renowned for being one of the largest and most heavily armed battleships ever built, Yamato was equipped with nine 18.1-inch guns and heavily armored to withstand significant naval engagements.

Learn More

Nagato

The IJN Nagato was a Japanese battleship launched in 1919 and the lead ship of her class in the Imperial Japanese Navy. As one of the first battleships designed with a focus on high firepower and speed, Nagato was armed with eight 16-inch guns and featured innovative design elements for her time.

Learn More

USS Alabama

The USS Alabama (BB-60) is a South Dakota-class battleship of the United States Navy, launched in 1942. Renowned for her heavy armor and firepower, Alabama was equipped with nine 16-inch guns and played a vital role in World War II.

Learn More

USS Iowa

The USS Iowa (BB-61) is an Iowa-class battleship of the United States Navy, launched in 1942. Known for her speed and formidable armament, Iowa featured nine 16-inch guns and a top speed of around 33 knots. She was also the known as the "The Big Stick".

Learn More

USS New Jersey

The USS New Jersey (BB-62) is an Iowa-class battleship of the United States Navy, launched in 1942. Known for her impressive size and firepower, she was one of the largest battleships ever built, equipped with nine 16-inch guns and capable of reaching speeds up to 33 knots.

Learn More

KMS Prinz Eugen

The Prinz Eugen was a German heavy cruiser launched in 1938, named after a famous 18th-century general. She was part of the Admiral Hipper class and featured advanced design and armament, including eight 8-inch guns. Prinz Eugen played a significant role in World War II, participating in the invasion of Denmark and Norway and later in the Atlantic, where she served alongside the battleship Bismarck.

Learn More

KMS Admiral Graf Spee

The Admiral Graf Spee was a German pocket battleship launched in 1934. Named after Admiral Maximilian von Spee, she played a prominent role in the early stages of World War II. Graf Spee was notable for her heavy armament and high speed, which allowed her to operate effectively as a commerce raider in the South Atlantic.

Learn More

KMS Scharnhorst

The Scharnhorst was a German battleship launched in 1936 and named after the Prussian general Gerhard von Scharnhorst. As part of the Scharnhorst-class battleships, she was notable for her fast speed, powerful armament, and innovative design.

About Us

FleetExhibit is an online platform dedicated to preserving the history of World War II naval vessels. Explore historical timelines, personal stories from veterans, and informative articles that celebrate the design, construction, and legacy of these iconic vessels, honoring those who served.
